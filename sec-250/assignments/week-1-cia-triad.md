# Week 1: CIA Triad

#### A common method of categorizing security issues is the CIA triad:  Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability. Which of these are affected if someone knows your campus login and password?  Describe the risk.

Confidentiality: If someone knows your login and password, then you lose all forms of confidentiality, because your data can be accessed (assuming you have no MFA). Your data can be exposed on the dark web or on social media.&#x20;

Availability: Theoretically, a user who knows your login and password can also change your password and backup emails. They can deny you access to your account, compromising the availability of the service.

Integrity: Access to your account typically allows the modification of data. Integrity revolves around the data being the same and correct. When an attacker modifies the data, that information is no longer correct. This means that an individual might be able to register for classes for you, compromising the integrity of your account.&#x20;

#### Do you think security cameras are a positive move or negative? **Explain**. Do some research and cite some examples of uses of security cameras in good or bad ways.&#x20;

Pre-Research: I believe security cameras can be beneficial at times, but they also have numerous negative effects. The pros of security cameras include increased monitoring and better crime catching, and possibly prevention. On the other hand, the knowledge of security cameras changes how we behave and can have detrimental effects on morale and the ability to express ourselves freely. Furthermore, in examples like Flock security cameras, individuals and vehicles can be falsely identified, resulting in false arrests and high-risk traffic stops of individuals who are innocent. Cameras can have security vulnerabilities and can be exposed to the internet, allowing anyone to see the cameras. This gives an individual the ability to stalk someone.

Post-research: Security cameras have not been proven effective at deterring crime, and they do not stop an individual from committing a crime. Abuse of security cameras, including stalking and blackmail, has occurred as a result of security cameras as well. Security cameras can be extremely useful when used correctly. They can help solve crimes if there is no other evidence or witnesses, and provide extremely useful leads. On the other hand, security cameras are expensive, may have security vulnerabilities, and can be accessed by malicious individuals.&#x20;

That being said, I think security cameras can be useful in specific situations and can be a highly effective tool at catching crime, but they can also be very damaging as well.&#x20;
