# Chapter 3

An organization needs to protect its goals through information security, achieved by following the CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, & Availability. Physical Security, Hardware Security, Software Security, and Network Security are all considered during this process. Human error is a large factor for security breaches, and employees should be trained on cybersecurity risks.

### Security Threats

There are many security threats that can endanger the information security of an organization. Internal Threats come from inside the organization, whereas External come from the outside.&#x20;

### Information Security Management Systems Framework (ISO 27001:2013)

A comprehensive information security guideline using control clauses, requiring risk assessment based on the needs of interested parties. Control clauses include: Information Security Policies, Organization of Information Security, Human Resources Security, Asset Management, Access Control, Cryptographic Controls, Physical and Environmental Security, Operations Security, Communications Security, System Acquisition, Development and Maintenance, Supplier Relationships, Information Security Incident Management, Information Security aspects of Business Continuity Management, Compliance.

### NIST Special Publication 800-39 + 800-53

The NIST Special Publication addresses how to handle information security and manage risk, adding specific guidelines on how to conduct risk assessments, assess, respond, and monitor that risk.&#x20;

### Parkerian Hexad

Three more descriptors on top of the CIA Triad: **possession, authenticity, and utility**.

Possession can lead to blackmail. Authenticity has to do with the genuineness of the information, and utility only considers a service available if it is usable.&#x20;
