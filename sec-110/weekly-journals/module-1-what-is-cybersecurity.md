### Vocabulary & Key Terms

Define important terms and acronyms related to the topic of the module.

#### Zero Trust

* The philosophy of not trusting users with access and providing only need-based access. (Authentication)

#### Authentication

* The process of figuring out the identity of a user.

#### Authorization

* The process of providing access to services (after Authentication, of course)

#### &#x20;Defense In Depth

* Having multiple cyber defense layers to stop cyberattacks and allow more time to respond to cyber threats.

#### &#x20;Confidentiality

* One of the key parts of the CIA triad. The idea is that systems should not disclose information to users who don’t need access.&#x20;

#### Integrity

* One of the key parts of the CIA triad.  The idea that systems should have as much integrity as possible, meaning that information should be accurate and correct.

#### &#x20;Availability

* One of the key parts of the CIA triad. The idea is that systems should be as available as possible. A cyber example: Servers should always be up and running to respond, as without them, you can’t access your data or service.&#x20;

#### Transparency

* The process of providing a user as much information about possible breaches that contain their information. A part of cyberethics.

#### Resource Allocation

* The process of balancing cybersecurity needs with the resources and limitations of the business. For example, a business should not pull 50% of its resources into cybersecurity if it is an extremely small company that doesn’t operate that much on the internet.&#x20;

#### Auditing

* The process of reviewing running systems to ensure that they follow regulatory requirements and comply with company guidelines.

### Main Ideas and Takeaways
