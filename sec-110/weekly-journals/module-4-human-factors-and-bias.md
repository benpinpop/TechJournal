### Vocabulary & Key Terms

Define important terms and acronyms related to the topic of the module.

#### Bias

* Prejudice against or in favor of one specific thing or grop, typically in a way that is unfair. Including, but not limited to:&#x20;
* Media Bias
* Motivational Bias
* Nonverbal Bias&#x20;
* Affinity Bias
* Halo/Horns Effect&#x20;
* Similarity Bias
* Contrast Effect&#x20;
* Attribution Bias
* Confirmation Bias
* Appearance Bias
* Conformity Bias
* Cognitive Bias

#### Social Engineering

* Manipulating people into giving up information or doing something that compromises their security.

#### Phishing

* Deceiving people into doing something/revealing sensitive information. (Via impersonation typically)
* Spear Phishing - Targeting specific individuals using a known sender
* Whaling - Targeting high-level targets (C-Suite) with phishing attacks.

#### Vishing

* Voice Phishing, also known as Vishing, is phishing over voice calls or text messages rather than email.

#### Pretexting

* Making a story to deceive a victim into giving out 9information

#### Baiting

* Leaving a phyiscal USB drive (infected) out, hoping someone will pick it up and plug it in.

#### Deepfakes

* Manipulating text, audio, or video to create a fake event, impersonating an individual.&#x20;
* For example, creating a video of Tom Cruise saying he’s starting a cryptocurrency.&#x20;

#### Open Source Intelligence (OSINT)

* Using open source data to find information on things or people
* Websites, Government Data Bases, Online Phone Books, etc
* Helpful for recon and attacker profiling, threat intelligence.

#### Digital Footprint

* Your data footprint on the internet. What things are Googleable about yourself? That’s your digital footprint.

### Main Ideas and Takeaways

Bullet points for notes that aren’t definitions, but explain how things work or why things are important. Feel free to bold/highlight the most important information.

* Bias affects everything in daily life, from the news media to how we interact with other people. Bias affects the way people think about things and decisions, specifically in Cybersecurity. Someone might have a motivational bias to get more work done, resulting in them cutting corners.&#x20;
* There are so many ways hackers exploit people to get them to hand over sensitive information. Phishing, Vishing (a form of social engineering) is incredibly effective if done properly, and we need to be very careful when going through daily activities.&#x20;
* Think before you click, use strong passwords, enable MFA, and beware of emails that try to scare you!
