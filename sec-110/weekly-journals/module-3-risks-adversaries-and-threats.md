### Vocabulary & Key Terms

Define important terms and acronyms related to the topic of the module.

#### Threat

* A potential/risk for damage as a result of an event or attack. (Natural Disaster, Human Error, Bad Actors)

#### Attack

* An intentional attempt to exploit a vulnerability maliciously.

#### Vulnerability

* A weakness in a system that exposes any sort of data or internal function that can be exploited by attackers.

#### &#x20;White Hat Hackers

* White Hat Hackers are ethical hackers who are hired/paid by companies to find and report vulnerabilities so they can be fixed.

#### &#x20;Hacktivists

* A hacktivist is a type of black hat hacker who gains access to systems to facilitate social or political change or raise awareness. Driven ideologically (Consider anonymous)

#### &#x20;Grey Hat Hackers

* Grey Hat Hackers are hackers who hack between the lines of ethical and unethical. They are hard to classify because their motivations might not be typical of your normal hacker.

#### Black Hat Hackers

* Black Hat Hackers are hackers who are malicious, attempting to exploit systems typically for financial gain.

#### Nation-States (APTs):

* APT, also known as an Advanced Persistent Threat, is a hacker type that has high levels of funding, typically from nation-states. They will not stop at just a firewall.

#### Blue Team

* Blue Teaming is a term used to describe people who defend against Red Teamers, ensuring systems stay secure and responding to attacks..

#### Red Team

* Red Teaming is a term used to describe penetration testers who attack companies to identify vulnerabilities.

#### Purple Team

* A term used to describe a team that helps communicate between two teams and figure out how to improve.

#### Green Hat Hacker

* A term for new or beginner hackers.

#### Insider/Outsider Threat

* Threats come from inside and outside. Insider Threats come from individuals working for the organization, whereas Outsider Threats come from outside an organization.

#### IOC

* Indicators of Compromise, evidence that an attack has happened on a system. These could come from anywhere, network scans, irregular network traffic, etc.&#x20;

#### Persistence

* A way for a hacker to stay on the system even if you restart it. The hackers might have installed a file to gain persistent access, so you have to remove their persistence mechanism.

#### Privilege Escalation

* Privilege Escalation is a way to gain more control or access to a system, like escalating to administrator or root privilege to implement malware. This is how a lot of hackers gain access and control to systems that they otherwise wouldn’t have access to in the first place.

#### C2

* Stands for Command and Control server used to control malware and botnets

#### Zero-Day

* A new security vulnerability not seen or known before. Because people don’t know about it, any hacker can use it without being detected by automated systems. Known as a zero-day because it’s in its 0th day of being discovered.
