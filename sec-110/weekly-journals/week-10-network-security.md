### Vocabulary & Key Terms

#### Passive Attacks

* Monitoring network traffic without changing it.

#### Packet Sniffing

* Capturing and watching data (Wireshark)

#### Traffic Analysis

* Analyzing traffic and communication patterns to understand a target.

#### Denial of Service (DoS)

* Overwhelming a network with traffic via a single device. Using up too much bandwidth on a device to stop other users from using it.

#### Distributed DoS (DDoS)

* A DoS attack, but with multiple devices, aka the network load is distributed over a variety of devices.

#### IP spoofing

* Faking your IP address to impersonate other devices

#### Session hijacking

* Taking over someone's active login session, like stealing their cookie and using it for your.

#### Social Engineering

* Attack via social factors, emailing someone to impersonate them to gain information.

#### Pretexting

* Pretending to be trustworthy to get valuable information.

#### Misconfiguration

* An application with wrong settings allows an attacker to access the device.&#x20;

#### Defense in Depth

* Multiple security controls in layers, like an onion.
  * Too many layers to stop an attacker.

#### MAC address spoofing

* Impersonating a MAC address

#### &#xD;ARP poisoning

* Changing router address tables

#### Routing attacks

* Targetting specific network devices to route you past a firewall

#### ICMP attacks

* Using ICMP like ping and tracert maliciously to gain information about a system.

#### Port scanning

* Scan ports on a machine to identify running and open services

#### TCP hijacking

* Hijacking a TCP connection to take over it.

#### UDP flooding

* UDP is one-way, so flooding (DoS) a service with meaningless requests.

#### Segmentation

* DMZ (Demilitarized Zone), separate public and private networks
* VLAN: Virtual Netowork Isolation

#### Firewall

* Filters packets, inspects and tracks connection states, allows and denies, and logs policies.

#### Intrusion Detection and Prevention (IDS)

* A monitoring system for your network to filter malicious network attacks.
  * Signature Based: Watching attack patterns
  * Host-Based: Watching certain systems
  * Anomaly-based detection: Identifies suspicious patterns

#### Wi-Fi Protected Access (WPA)

* WPA/2: Strong wireless protection
* WPA3: Latest wireless encryption/protection standard
