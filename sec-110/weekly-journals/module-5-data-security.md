### Vocabulary & Key Terms

#### Data

* Information is stored on devices either online or locally.
* Personal info, photos, messages, anything stored, shared, or used.

#### Data at rest

* Data that is being stored.
* Protect this with passwords and encryption

#### Data in transit

* Data that is actively being moved, like across the network, or uploading a file.
* Protect this with HTTPS, VPNs, and end-to-end encryption so hackers can’t see your data.

#### Encryption

* Changing data into a form that is unreadable, but that can be unlocked with a certain key or password
* Helpful for transferring data.

#### Full Backup

* A complete copy of data that is backed up to a storage device.
* Time-consuming, expensive
* Fastest restore time

#### Incremental Backup

* An incremental backup is a type of backup that backs up only the data that has changed since the last backup.&#x20;
* Increases backup speed and decreases storage space
* You have to restore each incremental backup from the last full backup to get back to your data if the data is corrupted. Time-consuming.

#### Differential Backup

* A differential backup is a type of backup that backs up the data that has changed since the last full backup.
* Imagine an incremental backup but compiled together.
* Differential backups can get really big over time.
* Much shorter restore time compared to incremental backups

### Main Ideas and Takeaways

Bullet points for notes that aren’t definitions, but explain how things work or why things are important. Feel free to bold/highlight the most important information.

* Your data is your own personal archive of yourself. It includes highly sensitive information about yourself, and can be used to impersonate you. If someone gets your personal information, they can steal accounts, money, and even blackmail you.
* Advertisers: Want to sell you things
* Hackers: Want to steal your data
* Companies: Want to understand what you do so they can make more money
* Data can be compromised a variety of ways, through phishing, malware, unsecured/unencrypted wifi, social engineering, and data breaches.
* Securing your data can be genuinely hard, but you can take steps to ensure it doesn’t get compromised. Use MFA and strong passwords, opt out of any data sharing or ensure all your privacy settings are on. Ensure you are using HTTPS and not HTTP, and watch out for phishing.
* Back up your data to ensure that no data loss ever occurs.
* Data Privacy Laws ensure that your data is protected, like GDPR, CCPA, and HIPAA. These laws have different jurisdictions, but you must be in compliance with these laws or you will face strict financial penalties.
